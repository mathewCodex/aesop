    1.1 Statutory warranty of conformity

    The French Consumer Code provides the following in terms of the statutory warranty of conformity:

    Article L. 217-4:

    “The seller is required to deliver goods that conform with the contract and is liable for any defects in conformity existing at the time of delivery. The seller is also liable for defects in conformity resulting from packaging, assembly instructions or installation if the seller was responsible for the installation under the contract or if the installation was carried out under its responsibility.”

    Article L. 217-5: “In order to conform with the contract, goods must:

    • Be fit for the purpose ordinarily expected of similar goods and, as applicable:

    • correspond to the description provided by the seller and possess the qualities the seller presented to the buyer in the form of a sample or model;

    • have the qualities a purchaser may legitimately expect in view of the public representations made by the seller, by the producer or by its representative, notably in advertisements or labelling;

    • Or, have the characteristics mutually agreed between the parties or be fit for any special use sought by the buyer and brought to the seller’s attention and which the seller accepted.

    Article L. 217-12: A suit resulting from a defect in conformity becomes time barred two years after the delivery of the goods.

    1.2 Warranty against hidden defects

    The French Civil Code provides as follows with respect to the warranty against hidden defects:

    Article 1641 of the French Civil Code: “The seller is bound by a warranty against hidden defects in the thing sold that render it unfit for its intended use, or that so diminish such use that the buyer would not have bought it, or would only have paid a lower price for it if had it known of the defects.”

    Article 1648 of the French Civil Code: “An action resulting from redhibitory defects must be brought by the buyer within two years of the discovery of the defect. In the case provided for by article 1642-1, the action must be brought, under penalty of being time barred, within the year that follows the date on which the seller can be discharged of apparent defects or apparent defects in conformity.”

    Within the framework of the warranty against hidden defects, Aesop undertakes to, after assessing the defect and at your option:

    • either reimburse you the full price of the returned product, or
